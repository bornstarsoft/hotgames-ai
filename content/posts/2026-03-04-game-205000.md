The Game Design and Implementation program at Capital Region BOCES stands out as a beacon for aspiring game developers, offering students a unique blend of technical skills and collaborative experiences. With nearly a decade of history, this program not only equips students with the coding know-how needed to create virtual games but also prepares them for a variety of roles in the booming gaming and tech industries.

Led by instructor Astra Wijaya, students like Michael Satterlee and Mikkail Warner are already diving deep into real-world projects, showcasing their creativity and technical prowess. As they transform board games into digital experiences, they gain insights into level design, programming, and even sound design, making this program a comprehensive introduction to the multifaceted world of game development.
