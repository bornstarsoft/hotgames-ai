Nagoshi Studio, founded by the acclaimed creator of the Yakuza series, Toshihiro Nagoshi, is reportedly facing closure due to a sudden withdrawal of funding from publisher NetEase. Just three months after the announcement of their debut title, Gang of Dragon, the studio has been informed that it will lose crucial financial support, leaving its future uncertain.

The decision by NetEase to cut off funding stems from the revelation that an additional 7 billion (approximately $44.4 million) is needed to complete the project. This news has left employees scrambling for alternative funding options, but as of now, no viable solutions have emerged. The situation highlights the precarious nature of game development financing, especially for new studios trying to establish themselves in a competitive market.

As the gaming community watches closely, the fate of Gang of Dragon hangs in the balance. Will Nagoshi be able to secure the necessary funds to continue this promising project, or will this mark the end of an era for a studio that aimed to deliver a fresh experience to fans? Only time will tell.
